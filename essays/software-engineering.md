## The day I switched majors

Like many other seniors in high school, I had no idea what I wanted to major in for college. My first thought was to do something in engineering, because I knew that I was good at math and would probably like to do something in the STEM field in the future. I decided to declare my major as Electrical Engineering and entered my first 3 semesters into college as an Electrical Engineering major. The first and last class I took for that major was EE 211. It was about 3-4 weeks into the semester and the class was pretty boring and we also had labs. It was once a week and by the time the third lab period came around I was ready to call it quits. I had NO IDEA what I was doing and would spend the whole 3 hour lab period trying to figure out how a breadboard worked. This class made me question what I was doing in Electrical Engineering and made me think if I wanted to do this for the rest of my career. It was after that lab day I started looking into other majors and I have no regrets since. I looked into and considered other STEM majors and it seemed like Computer Science was the best choice for me. After many emails sent and many meetings, I was declared as a Computer Science major.

## My first experience in ICS

The next semester, Spring 2020, I went to my first ICS class, ICS 111. It was the first class and I felt pretty nervous. Did I make the right decision? What if this major is not for me? Will I stay in college forever? That very first class quelled my fears. The professor went over a presentation on what Computer Science is and what the possibilities are with it. It was a COMPLETE flip from my former major. It really opened my eyes on how much computer science is involved in our lives and what you can do with it in the future, computer science is truly involved in all fields of careers. The class was great, I felt like I knew what I was doing and I actually looked forward going to class. This experience changed my life and I am so thankful that I made the decision to switch majors.

## The possible future

At the time of writing this essay I am in my junior year of college, taking ICS 314. I am interested in learning more about software engineering, artificial intelligence, and machine learning. I find it fascinating that machines can be programmed to think like humans and they can improve automatically through experience. I hope to do more side projects in the future, like learning how to make a mobile app, and making a bot to do things. I hope that through my college career, I become a better software engineer and computer scientist. I want to learn more coding languages, be a better programmer and make efficient code. I also look forward to getting internships so I could get more experience working in the work field. When I first started college, I had no direction. Now when I think about my future, there is are multiple paths I can see taking.
